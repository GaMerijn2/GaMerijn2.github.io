Introduction

In the project Operation Starfall we made a new enemy called Scopi.
We had to convert the scopi prefab into a BaseEnemy variant to be able to use it in game and make it interact with the environment.

Behaviour

This is Scopi.
He's one of the enemies in the game Operation Starfall and its the enemy a classmate and I have been working on.

We had to remake the enemy's attack states from code to the StateMachine and convert him from just code to a whole enemy as a enemy prefab in the game that could be used in the game's systems.
at first you couldn't hit and damage him, so he was invincible.

We had to fix some bugs on the way and it took like 2/3 weeks to complete him.

You are able to hit Scopi and get him stuck in a magnetic platform to you can use him to destroy walls you normally cannot destroy and he is basicly a death ray that demolishes everything in his path.

Behaviour

Scopi doesn't move in the game by itself, because he is a stationairy enemy

When he finds a target to attack he rotates towards the target and starts to charge his laser. If the laser is fully charged he lets out a strong beam that can destroy everything in his path and you will get hit if you touch the laser's beam.

You can make him stop shooting by hitting him once so he gets in the fuzzy state and stops shooting so you can kill him if you're fast enough.

Behaviour

On the right you can see the StateMachine of a Scopi. In this graph you can see nodes that have scripts attached to them that handle the behaviour of the enemy the StateMachine is for.

The nodes have a Input (OnEnter) and a output (OnExit). These can be used to trigger different types of behaviour that the attached script has.

This way you can fully customize the behaviour of a enemy, and it's easy to change if needed.
